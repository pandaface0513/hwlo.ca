Anatroc - A Virtual Reality Puzzle Adventure

Design Concept:

Anatroc is an immersive virtual reality puzzle adventure created for the Archiact Interactive Global Game Jam. Designed to test player's exploration skills, visual memory, and navigation skills as players explore the winding pathways of the maze. Decipher clues to uncover hidden pathways and perhaps even an exit. The original design of Anatroc was to create an immersive virtual reality experiences with two players cooperating with one another. To achieve this, the team researched the existing titles for Google Cardboard virtual reality headset. We foundo out that while there are a few PvP multiplayer games exist, but there were no cooperative multiplayer virtual reality titles available. We wanted to design Anatroc as the first cooperative multiplayer virtual reality experiences.

In its completion, Anatroc became a first person maze exploration game with both players taking uniqe roles. One player would take on the role of the maze avatar and would be forced to navigate the game's maze. The other palyer would take on the role of the first player's navigational AI. This player cannot move but has full access to a map of the maze. This map displays both players' locations and maze's doors and exit. Anatroc's gameplay will be formed through verbal communication between the two players in order to complete the maze. Both players are required to exchange clues in order to proceed in game levels. For example, the maze player will communicate a hint back to the AI player, for the AI player to unlock hidden doorways for maze player to explore. This game concept is based on numerous real-life escape room games in Vancouver, which I have personally researched on.

My roles in Anatroc

In Anatroc, I took on the role of the lead programmer. For my first step in developing the game, I started to experiment with the Google Cardboard SDK with Unity. I was responsibe for majority of the game mechanics found in the maze. This includes but not limited to the movement mechanic, raycasting mechanic, real-time minimap mechanic. I also used the scene loader to minimize the loads the device has to do. The experience was very valuable, as I learned in the progress, that it is much different to to design a game for desktop versus design a game for mobile. As mobile devices are not as powerful or as resourceful traditional computers, virtual reality games for mobile requires much delicate attentions to details. I spent several days finding ways to optimise Anatroc to run in reasonable framerates for mobile phones. I also worked with another programer in collaboration to create the virtual reality experiences.

Outcome

As a result Anatroc was selected as one of the 20 game jam finalists from over 100 individual submissions. We also finished in 4th place in the "player's choice" voting portion of the contest. In reflection I maintain that it was the right decision to redesign Anatroc as a single player game given the time contraints. However, our team agree that Anatroc would have been a much more interesting and immersive experience with the multiplayer gameplay aspect intact. In summary I felt that competing in this game jam was a valuable experience in learning to design in virtual reality, and also in completing a project under a strict deadline. Perhaps it would be worth while to reapproach it.
